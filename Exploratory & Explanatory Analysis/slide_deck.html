Bay Wheels User Trends in 2018¶

by Laila Madany¶

Investigation Overview¶

The primary goal of this investigation was looking at the different behaviors and patterns of Bay Wheels users. Bay Wheels, previously Ford GoBike, is a regional public bicycle sharing system in California's San Francisco Bay Area. The main focus was on the start timestamp (Time, Day & Month), figuring out the peak times when bikes are high in demand as well as off-peak times.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted information about individual approximately 1,860,000 trips made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area in 2018. The attributes included start time, trip duration, user type as well as additional measurements such as gender and birth date. 147,414 data points were removed from the analysis due to missing information and validity issues.

User Types¶

As defined on the Bay Wheels Website, “Subscriber” are Member of Bay Wheels whereas “Customer” are casual users. In 2018, Subscribers contributed with the majority of trips making around 88.4%. The remaining 11.6% trips were related to customers.

Average Weekly Trip Duration By User Type¶

The average trip duration for Customers is longer than that of Subscribers throughout the whole week. Subscibers have a relatively unchanging trip durations which might be due to work/school schedules but it increases during the weekend. For Customers, their average trip duration drastically jumps on Saturday. In general for both user types, trips are shorter during weekdays and longer during weekends.

Hourly Trend during Weekdays By User Type¶

Each user type have different patterns and exhibit different preferences. Subscibers depict the behavior of school/work commuters where they used the service mostly during weekdays at normal start and end working hours. On the other hand, customers are more causual and flexible renting bikes on weekends.

Subscribers: The highest number of trips are concentrated during weekdays from Monday to Friday, mainly during 8AM and 5 PM. On weekends, the numbers of trips significantly dropped and were more around the afternoon on these days. Overall Peak: Tuesday 8 AM

Customers: They are using the service most often during the weekends from around 12 AM to 3-4 PM - that's when the number of trips are highly concentrated. On weekdays, they tend to bike mostly at 5 PM. Overall Peak: Saturday 2 PM & Friday 5 PM.

Monthly Trend Line By User Types¶

It does not come as a surprise that both Customers and Subscribers tend to use the bike service during early-mid autumn and summer months. During winter months, the demand was quite low which is quite expected given the freezing cold weather. Januaray is considered to be the coldest month in San Francisco Reference Link.

The number of trips by subscribers significantly increased during May as well as October and drastically dropped starting November. For customers, the peak month has been July. Unlike subscribers, their number of trips decreased during October.

Extra: Monthly Trend Line By Age Groups¶

At the beginning of 2018, the 30-40 age group had the highest number of trips. It remained occupying the highest monthly trip count up until June. This is when the turning point happened, the 18-30 age group took the lead and kept it until the end of the year. The 18-30 age group has been increasing month by month from Jan till May with almost the same numbers as the 30-40 age group but what suddenly happened during June?
